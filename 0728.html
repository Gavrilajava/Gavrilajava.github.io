While studying JavaScript, I have seen three ways to declare variables, namely var, let and const. In this article I will try to summarize the differences between these assignment operators. To really understand the difference between var, let and const, we need to understand the following concepts:
  • The declaration of a variable
  • Initializing a variable
  • Scope of visibility
  • The mechanism of hoisting ("raising")
Declaring a Variable Declaring a variable is the process of introducing a new definition into a program or, to be more precise, into its scope. In JavaScript, variables are initially undefined when we declare the keyword var (this is automatically done by the interpreter).
